Pirl Masternode Setup Tutorial

Pirl Masternode Setup Tutorial
  • pirl
  • setup

  • beta

Overview

Setting up a Pirl masternode involves a few things. You'll need to accumulate/acquire/purchase 20000 Pirl which will be used as collateral. You will need to place this collateral in a stand-alone wallet. To do this, you'll use the Pirl Natuilus wallet, which can be installed on your normal workstation.
You'll also create an account at Pirl's Poseidon website to manage your masternode.
To prove that you are the owner of that address, you'll need to send a small amount of Pirl to a known address. This will register the transaction in the blockchain, proving that you own the address.
Once these pieces are in place, you can use your Nautilus account to register/watch the Pirl Masternode contract and deposit your 20000 pirl into the contracts wallet. Don't worry. You can retrieve that deposit (stake) at any time by de-activating your masternode. The Pirl will be returned to your Nautilus wallet address.
Finally, you will set up a linux workstation, download the Pirl masternode code, perform a simple configuration, and run the masternode. You can monitor the masternode's status by viewing your Poseidon account. Similarly, you can monitor payouts by monitoring the transactions to your Nautilus wallet address.

Prerequisites

  • Download and Install the Nautilus Wallet on your normal computer - Download Here
  • Accumulate/acquire/purchase 20,000 Pirl (Plus a tiny amount for a validation transaction) that will be used as collateral.
  • A dedicated server or VPS (Virtual Private Server) - Linux operating system, 4GB memory, 20GB disk space, 3TB data transfer.
    The VPS must support a public IP address. Port 30303, both UDP and TCP need to be opened in your firewall.
  • Create an account at Pirl Poseidon. Choose a strong password!

-- STEP 1 --
Create An Identifying Transaction For Your Collateral Address

We need to send a transaction from the address that your 20,000 Pirl is being held in. This is to confirm your ownership of the address :
1/. Login to Poseidon, and on the left hand menu, choose Wallet -> Wallet details. You will see a wallet address for your Pirl Account. Copy this address.
2/. Go to Nautilus, and send a transaction from the address your collateral is held in to your Poseidon address. Any amount will do (1 or even 0.5 pirl)

IMPORTANT : DO NOT SEND YOUR 20,000 PIRL TO POSEIDON !!! DO NOT SEND FROM AN EXCHANGE WALLET !!!

You will not lose this Pirl, it will be completely accessible to you in your Poseidon account.
3/. Copy the transaction hash from, you will need it in the next step.



 

-- STEP 2 --
Create Masternode

1/. Go back to Poseidon and choose from the left menu Masternodes -> Create masternode.


2/. Under "Create a new masternode" fields, enter :

  • Name*: Your masternode name, you can put whatever you want,
  • Wallet id*: Enter the wallet address your collateral is in. (Your Nautilus address)
  • Tx hash validation*: Enter the saved transaction hash from step 1

3/. Click on "Create".


You will now see this masternode entry that you created under "My Masternodes".

-- STEP 4 --
Starting The Masternode

1/. Log into the VPS or dedicated server that you are using for your masternode.
2/. Verify that a public IP address has been assigned to the server and that the appropriate ports have been opened (see Prerequisites section)
3/. Upload the node binary to this account which can be found in the link below (use the wget linux command or ftp to transfer it to the VPS):
http://release.pirl.io/downloads/masternode/linux/pirl

Before running the node, you need two more pieces of information :
4/. Go to your Poseidon account. On the top left of the screen, click on you username, and select "settings"
You will see on the right : "Your TOKEN is : 0xxxx..." this is your Dashboard token. Copy this token


5/. In the left menu, click on "Masternodes" --> "My masternodes"
You will see on the right a token code for your masternode. This is your masternode token. Copy this Token

6/. Now that you have these two tokens, go back to your VPS, type the following into the linux terminal (keep the quotes):
( Fictitious example: )

export TOKEN="1a23456b7c890de01fg2h34567h8ij90k12l3mn45"  
export MASTERNODE="a1234567-90a1-2b34-56e7-1234f5dfe6cb"

 

MASTERNODE is your token from point 5/. and TOKEN your token from point 4/.

7/. After that, all you need to do is be in the same directory that you uploaded the pirl binary into, and enter in a terminal :

 

./pirl

And it will start right up! If you get a permissions error when trying to execute it, type this into the terminal, and execute ./pirl again

chmod 755 pirl

After a brief initialization (a few minutes), the terminal should scroll with update messages every ~10 seconds.


Nice, your masternode is running !

To further verify that the Masternode is functioning, log into your Poseidon account, click My Master Nodes and then click the Details button. You should see a growing list of transactions. The transaction list will automatically refresh, showing the most-recent trasnactions.
 

 

--Troubleshooting--

On the linux server/masternode, the 'Nice your Masternode Is Connected' status is not appearing

  • Double check that the EXPORT statements were run and that the dashboard and masternode tokens are recorded properly
  • Verify that your firewall meets the conditions set in the 'Prerequisites' section of this document
  • Verify that you downloaded the correct Pirl binary/code. You may be using an outdated version.


My VPS is running properly, but the Poseidon / Masternode / Details view does not show any blocks/transactions

  • Double check that the EXPORT statements were run and that the dashboard and masternode tokens are recorded properly
  • Verify that your IP address has been registered and assigned to the masternode server (or VPS, in most cases). It's worth nothing that certain VPS providers do not permit the direct assignment of the IP address to the virtual server. Several users that attempted to run a Pirl miner using AWS EC2 instances have reported problems.
  • Verify that you downloaded the correct Pirl binary/code. You may be using an outdated version.



How do I monitor payouts

  • It's easiest to monitor payouts by monitoring transactions to your Nautilus wallet address.
  • Using a web browser, simply open https://pirl.site and input your Nautilus wallet address

     

Deactivating Your Masternode

If you wish to deactivate your masternode, and put your 20,000 Pirl back into your wallet address, you need to go back into the contract that you set up in Oystr previously. Where you had last chosen “Node Registration”, you can also see on the drop down box “Disable Node”. Proceed with executing “Disable Node”, and after that, do the same for “Withdraw Stake”. Your node will now be deactivated, and your collateral back in your wallet address.
